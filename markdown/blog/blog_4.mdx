# Celebrating Oromo Culture: Our Annual Cultural Show

Culture is the heartbeat of community. For the Oromo diaspora in British Columbia, preserving and sharing our heritage is both an act of resilience and a bridge to our Canadian neighbours. At OCRSS, our annual cultural show has become one of the most anticipated events of the year—a vibrant gathering where generations come together to celebrate Oromo music, dance, traditional dress, and cuisine while welcoming friends from across Metro Vancouver and beyond.

## A Day of Music, Dance, and Connection

The cultural show features performances by Oromo artists and youth groups, traditional coffee ceremonies, and displays of handwoven textiles and crafts. Elders share stories and histories, ensuring that younger generations growing up in Canada remain connected to their roots. At the same time, the event introduces newcomers and the broader public to the richness of Oromo culture, fostering mutual understanding and strengthening the fabric of our multicultural society.

Key highlights of our annual celebration include:

- Traditional Oromo dances performed by community groups and youth
- Live music and drumming showcasing regional styles
- Traditional Oromo coffee ceremony (Bunna Qalaa) and cuisine
- Displays of Oromo dress, art, and cultural artefacts
- Speeches and storytelling by community elders and leaders

## Building Bridges Through Culture

Beyond celebration, the cultural show serves as a platform for building connections. Many newcomers who attend find comfort in seeing their culture honoured in their new home. Local politicians, settlement workers, and neighbours who attend leave with a deeper appreciation of the Oromo community and the contributions we make to British Columbia. We invite you to join us at our next annual cultural show—whether you are Oromo, a supporter of refugee resettlement, or simply curious to learn more. Together, we build a community where everyone belongs.
