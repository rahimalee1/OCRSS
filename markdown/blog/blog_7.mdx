# Newcomer Success Stories: From Arrival to Independence

Behind every statistic about refugee resettlement are individual stories of resilience, hope, and hard work. At OCRSS, we have had the privilege of walking alongside hundreds of families as they navigate the journey from arrival to independence. While every story is unique, they share common threads: initial uncertainty, gradual adaptation, and ultimately, the pride of building a new life in Canada. Here we highlight a few of the success stories that inspire our work every day.

## Navigating the First Year

The first twelve months in Canada are often the most challenging. Families must secure housing, enrol children in school, access healthcare, and begin learning the language—all while processing the experiences that led them to flee. With support from OCRSS and our partners, many families report feeling more confident and connected by the end of that first year. One mother of three shared that our housing support meant she could focus on her children's education instead of worrying about where they would sleep. Another client, a former teacher, completed language classes and secured a position as a teaching assistant within eighteen months of arrival.

Common milestones we celebrate with families include:

- Securing stable housing and completing the first year of tenancy
- Parents obtaining employment in their field or in related roles
- Children excelling in school and participating in extracurricular activities
- Family members achieving language proficiency and passing citizenship exams
- Graduating from direct settlement support to giving back as volunteers or mentors

## Building a Future in British Columbia

Success is not measured solely by employment or housing—it is also measured by the sense of belonging that families develop over time. Many of our clients have become active members of their neighbourhoods, volunteering at schools, participating in community events, and supporting newer arrivals. Their journeys remind us that with the right support, refugees do not merely survive; they thrive. We are grateful to every family who has trusted us with their story and to every supporter who has made these successes possible. If you are a newcomer reading this, we hope these stories offer hope and reassurance that your dreams for a better life in Canada are within reach.
